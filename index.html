The Global Impact of Smoking

Did you know that smoking is the second highest death risk factor in the world, killing more than 8 million people every year. More than 7 million of these recorded deaths are due to direct use of tobacco, while approximately 1.2 million people die globally due to exposure from second-hand smoke.

Deaths recorded in millions by Risk Factor, 2019

Smoking in the 20th Century

Over the 20th century, tobacco smoking caused approximately 100 million deaths globally. According to the IHME(Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation), about 13% of global deaths is due to direct tobacco smoking, while about 2% is due to second hand smoke

Smokers Demographics

Approximately, each year around 50% of people who die from smoking are aged over 70, while 40% are aged between 50-69 and only about 10% of recorded deaths is attributed to people aged between 15 and 49.
